## 1. Aidan — PM2.5 / PM10 / VOCs briefing (~35 slides)

**Framing.** Pivots from all-South-Texas to Coastal Bend scope; then dives into
the FRM/FEM distinction, method-code semantics, and per-sensor timelines that
were missing from our prior time-series paper. Explicitly designed so we can
carry these details straight into the methods section of the next paper.

**Highlights**

- **PM2.5 sensor rollout 2010–2025** — TEOM (TM 1400) lost FEM status ~2018 and
  wasn't re-designated until the TM 1405 five years later. That 5-yr gap is a
  live question: do we use TM 1400 data during its non-FEM window? Literature
  reports systematic 10 %–30 % pre/post-swap concentration differences at
  monitor changes.
- **Suggested approach for method-swap bias:** train ML models on the seasonal
  decomposition *residuals* / extracted-trend components rather than raw
  concentrations — sidesteps some of the level-shift artefacts.
- **CC Holly PM10 discontinuity** — filter method 141 (2015–2018) → 5-yr gap →
  BAM method 639 (2024–2025). Explicit warning: do **not** model as a
  continuous series.
- **Aidan's 88101 vs 88502 fix** — earlier merge in the concatenation step had
  mixed these together in some rows; database is now clean.
- **VOC 1-hr vs 24-hr requery decision** — original ingest queried 1-hr first
  and only fell back to 24-hr where 1-hr returned nothing. Worth going back
  and re-querying 24-hr for sites where 1-hr was empty to confirm nothing was
  missed.
- **Dr. Melaram's methodological point:** almost no air-pollution health paper
  in the literature discusses instrumentation changes. The Houston longitudinal
  study we cited previously did not. This is a deliberate direction our lab
  is pushing.

## 2. Jasmine — SO₂ + VOCs briefing

**SO₂ measurement**

- **Method:** UV Fluorescence (UVF). Reference instrument Thermo TEI 43i.
- **Method codes in our data:** 92 (continuous automated UVF) and 100
  (continuous instrumental UVF) — both FEM.
- **NAAQS history:** primary 75 ppb 1-hr since 2010, retained through 2024.
  Secondary tightened from 500 ppb 3-hr to **10 ppb annual (3-yr avg)** in
  2025 — worth understanding the rationale.

**Weather-influence table (the meeting's most-praised artefact).** For each
pollutant, a table of meteorological factor × effect direction × **plain-English
mechanism**. Cross-comparison of ozone vs SO₂ was the moment Dr. Melaram flagged
that pollutants can have *opposite* relationships to the same meteorological
driver (solar radiation ↑ ozone but ↓ SO₂, via photolysis). Aidan's feedback
verbatim: *"I love this table. The 'what happens specifically' column — this is
what's extra special to what you're bringing to the table."*

**Boundary layer height (BLH).** Extended discussion mid-briefing. Jasmine
covered the mixing-layer concept, thunderstorm formation, and coastal-breeze
influence. Dr. Melaram tied BLH to acute-pollen health effects. **This
discussion directly seeded the 2026-07-15 pollution-rose pivot.**

**VOCs.** Parts-per-billion measurements across ~46 species at CB sites.
Limitation flagged: differentiation between closely-related VOC species is
difficult given the closely-clustered analytical response.

**AQI-color categorical ML target idea.** Instead of predicting continuous
pollutant concentrations, predict AQI-color bins ("green / yellow / red
tomorrow given the forecast"). Framed as a family-actionable output rather
than a regulatory metric. Team loved it; will revisit in follow-up meetings.

## 3. Manasa — Ozone + CO briefing (~35 slides)

**Ozone chemistry.** Secondary pollutant: NOₓ + VOCs + heat + UV → O₃.
Ground-level ≠ stratospheric ozone — regulatory framing very different.

**Measurement principles**

- **Dominant (~99 % of monitors):** UV photometric absorption at 254 nm using
  Beer–Lambert law. This is FEM.
- **FRM:** gas-phase chemiluminescence with ethylene (older) or NO — historically
  the reference but expensive + hazardous, so UV took over.
- **Method codes in our data:** 56, 87, 187 — all FEM.

**NAAQS history 1971–current.** Current standard: 0.070 ppm 8-hr, 3-yr avg of
the annual 4th-highest daily max. Both primary and secondary set at the same
level; 2020 review retained the value.

**Cross-standard comparison table (EPA vs WHO).** WHO uses mg/m³ and has an
A/B tolerance-days framework; EPA uses ppm and different averaging windows.
Worth having as a supplementary table.

**TCEQ vs EPA data-source table** (unanimously flagged as its own
publishable perspective piece):

| Aspect | TCEQ | EPA (AQS) |
|---|---|---|
| Data grain | Raw 1-hr averages | 8-hr daily max |
| Refresh | Every hour, near real-time | Months of latency |
| Certification | Uncertified | Certified |
| Units | ppb | ppm |

Dr. Melaram directly to Aidan: *"You could do a commentary or a perspective
paper. That's a paper in itself. You could easily pump out a paper while
still streamlining processes for the time-series component."*

**Exceedance-days trend.** Nueces County ozone exceedance days trending down
since 2003; post-2019 threshold is a maximum of 3 days for a year to still
count as a "good year".

**CO.** No CO monitors anywhere in the Coastal Bend region — all TCEQ CO
sites are in San Antonio (Bexar). Manasa's recommendation: **drop CO from
the first manuscript**, keep the gap statement in [pollutants/co.md](../pollutants/co.md)
as a scope note. Team agreed.
