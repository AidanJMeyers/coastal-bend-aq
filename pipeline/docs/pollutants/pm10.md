# PM10 — Coarse Particulate Matter

> **Lead:** Aidan Meyers
> **Source deck:** [`briefings/2026-07-01_AM_PM_VOC_briefing.pptx`](../briefings/2026-07-01_AM_PM_VOC_briefing.pptx) (slides 30–39)
> **Coastal Bend data:** sparse — CC Holly (483550034) is the primary; ~16,600 rows total (much of it 1-in-6 day filter era pre-2019)
> **Neon:** `aq_coastal_bend.pollutant_hourly WHERE pollutant_group='PM10'`

Coastal Bend PM10 coverage is thin — TCEQ historically prioritized
PM2.5, ozone, and VOCs over PM10 in this region. But **PM10 stays
scientifically important**: sea salt + Saharan dust + Port/Ship Channel
fugitive dust deliver a coarse-fraction burden with distinct health
mechanisms that PM2.5 measurement can't resolve.

## 1. Definition

**PM10** = airborne particulate matter with **aerodynamic diameter ≤ 10 µm** —
the "thoracic fraction," small enough to penetrate past the larynx
([40 CFR Part 50, Appendix J](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-40/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-50/appendix-Appendix%20J%20to%20Part%2050)).

PM10 is not a single population — it's the sum of two overlapping modes:

- **Fine mode (≤ 2.5 µm)** = PM2.5 (secondary aerosol, combustion) —
  see [PM2.5 deep-dive](./pm25.md)
- **Coarse mode (2.5–10 µm)** = mechanical + biological (crustal dust,
  sea salt, pollen)

Coarse-mode chemistry differs sharply from PM2.5:

| Mode | Dominant chemistry | Coastal Bend source |
|---|---|---|
| Crustal | Al, Si, Ca, Fe, K | Windblown dust (local + Saharan) |
| Sea salt | Na, Cl, Mg, sulfate | Bubble-bursting → Coastal Bend baseline |
| Biological | Pollen, spores, endotoxin, bacteria | Vegetation + Saharan cargo |

**Respiratory deposition by size:**

| Region | Size fraction | Health path |
|---|---|---|
| Nose / pharynx | PM10 (2.5–10 µm) | Mucociliary clearance; upper-airway inflammation |
| Trachea / bronchi | PM10-2.5 mixed | COPD + asthma exacerbation; bronchial irritation |
| Alveoli / systemic | PM2.5 + UFP | Cardiovascular + systemic inflammation |

## 2. Sources — dominant in the Coastal Bend

- **Sea salt aerosol** — bubble-bursting + jet-drop production in
  whitecaps; Coastal Bend's prevailing SE Gulf wind delivers near-
  continuous sea salt. Mediterranean + Gulf coastal studies show > 50%
  of summer PM10 mass at coastal receptors can be sea salt.
- **Windblown mineral dust** — emission scales as u*³ above threshold
  friction velocity (~5–7 m/s at 10 m for undisturbed soils). Local
  dust from South Texas agricultural belt (grain sorghum, cotton,
  corn). Multi-year La Niña drought (2020–2025) has increased soil
  erodibility and dust event frequency.
- **Trans-Atlantic Saharan dust** — June–August peak;
  [Bozlaker et al. 2019, *JGR-Atmos*](https://doi.org/10.1029/2019JD030702)
  quantified an 85% dust increase in Houston/Galveston PM10 during
  the July 2008 Saharan intrusion. Same air mass reaches Coastal Bend.
  Cargo is biological + mineral — endotoxin, fungal spores,
  respirable metals ([Bozlaker et al. 2024, *ES&T*](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.3c07156)).
- **Road dust** — paved-road fraction dominates urban PM10 in Ship
  Channel corridor. Unpaved-road dust more relevant to rural Kleberg /
  San Patricio / Jim Wells (all outside our active-monitor footprint).
- **Port + Ship Channel fugitive dust** — bulk material handling
  (petroleum coke, aggregate, fertilizer), unpaved industrial yards,
  heavy diesel traffic.
- **Sea-salt-derived secondary chemistry** — aging sea salt reacts
  with anthropogenic sulfate + nitrate to produce HCl, ClNO2, and
  reactive halogens.

## 3. Saharan dust — the biggest episodic driver

**Trans-Atlantic transport:**
[Prospero & Lamb 2003, *Science*](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1089915)
— Barbados-measured African dust 1965–1998 strongly anti-correlated
with Sahelian rainfall (drying → dust).
[Yu et al. 2015, *RSE*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rse.2015.01.010) —
CALIPSO lidar quantified Sahara exports ~182 Mt of dust/year over the
western edge; ~132 Mt reaches the Atlantic; a fraction reaches the
Gulf of Mexico.

**Peak Coastal Bend impact:** June–August (coincident with maximum
African easterly wave activity and the mature Saharan Air Layer).
Multi-day pulses — a single event can double coastal daily PM10 for
2–4 days.

**Health signal:**
[Ortiz-Martínez et al. 2015, *Mediators Inflamm*](https://doi.org/10.1155/2015/784212)
— Puerto Rico pediatric asthma claims tracked average PM10, PM10
increment, and African Dust Event days. Mechanism: inhaled
endotoxin + fungal β-glucans → TLR-4 / TLR-2 airway inflammation →
asthma exacerbation. Directly transferable to Gulf Coast during
summer Saharan intrusions.

**Coupled hurricane suppression:**
[Prospero & Mayol-Bracero 2013, *BAMS*](https://doi.org/10.1175/BAMS-D-12-00142.1)
— Saharan dust suppresses tropical cyclogenesis via SAL dry-air
entrainment, dust radiative cooling of MDR SSTs, and mid-level trade
wind inversion strengthening. **Heavy dust weeks reduce local
hurricane risk while simultaneously delivering a health-relevant
PM10 burden.** This coupling matters for climate-attribution analyses
of PM10 trends.

## 4. Health effects — coarse-fraction distinct pathway

- **Respiratory hospital admissions** —
  [Adar et al. 2014 systematic review](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40572-014-0022-z):
  PM10-2.5 (coarse) associations with respiratory hospital admissions
  are "as strong or stronger than for PM2.5" — survives publication-
  bias adjustment. Asthma and COPD endpoints most consistent.
- **Cardiovascular (weaker than PM2.5)** —
  [AHA 2010 statement (Brook et al.)](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0b013e3181dbece1):
  evidence for PM10-2.5 cardiovascular effect is "suggestive" rather
  than causal. [Malig & Ostro 2009, *OEM*](https://doi.org/10.1136/oem.2008.045393)
  found California associations concentrated in Hispanic populations.
- **Biological cargo — dust + asthma** — Ortiz-Martínez 2015 (above);
  [Bozlaker 2024 *ES&T*](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.3c07156)
  documented respirable metals, bacteria, fungi in Houston Saharan dust.
- **Windborne fungi (regional)** —
  [Tong et al. 2017, *GRL*](https://doi.org/10.1002/2017GL073524):
  PM10 links to Valley Fever timing in Arizona. Coccidioidomycosis
  rare in Coastal Bend, but the mechanism (windborne spore transport
  in coarse fraction) is analogous to Aspergillus / Alternaria during
  local dust events.

## 5. NAAQS — one standard remains, one was revoked

**Current standard:** [150 µg/m³ 24-hr](https://www.epa.gov/pm-pollution/national-ambient-air-quality-standards-naaqs-pm)
(primary + secondary, identical) — **not to be exceeded more than
once per year on average over three consecutive years.**

**What was REVOKED:** Annual 50 µg/m³ PM10 standard (1987 → 2006).
EPA's 2006 rationale (upheld by the D.C. Circuit):

1. Epidemiological evidence for chronic effects of coarse PM (as
   distinct from PM2.5) was weak;
2. PM10 as an indicator was "poorly matched" to the coarse fraction
   because it also included PM2.5.

The annual PM2.5 standard now covers chronic fine-particle exposure;
the retained 24-hr PM10 standard covers episodic thoracic coarse
exposures.

**Exceptional Events Rule** — the "once per year on average" form
matters in the Coastal Bend. [40 CFR 50.14](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-40/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-50/section-50.14)
allows states to petition to exclude natural-event exceedances from
regulatory determinations. Texas has historically flagged Saharan
dust intrusions under this provision — a 24-hr > 150 µg/m³ dust event
doesn't automatically drive a violation.

## 6. Measurement methods

### 6.1 FRM — Appendix J (24-hr integrated)

24-hr integrated sample on a filter through a 10-µm size-selective
inlet. Gravimetric mass determination at STP (25 °C, 1 atm — different
from PM2.5 which is LC).

Reference samplers: Andersen SA-2000 (hi-vol), Wedding & Associates
PM10 hi-vol, BGI PQ200 (small vol, low-flow), Thermo Partisol 2025i,
Graseby GMW-321.

[Appendix O](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-40/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-50/appendix-Appendix%20O%20to%20Part%2050)
also defines a PM10-2.5 (coarse fraction) reference method using
paired dichotomous FRMs as a subtraction.

### 6.2 Continuous FEM

- **Met One BAM 1020** — first PM10 FEM designation 1998
  ([63 FR 41253](https://www.federalregister.gov/citation/63-FR-41253);
  EQPM-0798-122). Same β-attenuation physics as PM2.5 BAM but with a
  10-µm cyclone inlet.
- **Thermo/R&P TEOM 1400ab / 1405** — semi-volatile loss less severe
  for coarse mode (drier, less volatile), so TEOM PM10 designations
  survived even where PM2.5 TEOM was withdrawn.
- **Optical (GRIMM, Thermo 5030 SHARP)** — size-resolved by laser
  scattering; cover PM10 + PM2.5 + PM1 in a single instrument.

**Low-cost sensors: NOT suitable for PM10.** PurpleAir and similar
underestimate coarse fraction — sensor optical response saturates
below ~1 µm.
[Kaur & Kelly 2023, *AMT*](https://doi.org/10.5194/amt-16-1311-2023)
documented 5–6× underestimation during dust events. **Do not
substitute.**

| Param | Meaning | Coastal Bend presence |

| 85101 | PM10 LC | Rare |

| Method | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|

**Units note:** STP (25 °C, 1 atm) vs LC (local ambient) differ by
only ~1.7% for a 30 °C Coastal Bend ambient (small but not zero).
Most PM10 rows in our pipeline are STP-referenced.

## 8. CC Holly PM10 discontinuity — the poster child

CC Holly (483550034) has a **fundamental methodological discontinuity**:

| Period | Method code | Sampling | Rows/year |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2015–2018 | 141 | 1-in-6 day filter (hi-vol FRM) | ~60–120 |
| 2019–2023 | — | **NO PM10 DATA** | 0 |
| 2024–2025 | 639 | Continuous BAM | ~8,300 |

**Do not model as a continuous series.** The 2015–2018 filter era
and the 2024–2025 BAM era are effectively different instruments
measuring different sampling windows. Any trend analysis must
segment.

## 9. Weather relationships

| Variable | Direction | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Wind speed** | + (STRONG positive) | Emission ∝ u*³ above threshold. High-wind fronts + dryline generate largest local excursions. |
| **Wind direction** | ± (source attribution) | SE = marine + Saharan; N/NW = agricultural/rangeland |
| **Precipitation** | − (STRONG negative) | Below-cloud impaction scavenges coarse particles efficiently — 40-80% drop after rain |
| **Relative humidity** | ± | Sea salt hygroscopic growth at RH > 70% inflates + shortens residence time |
| **ENSO (La Niña → dust)** | + | 2020–2025 Southern Plains drought under consecutive La Niña years drove increased dust; see [NOAA 2025 State of the Climate](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/monitoring/monthly-report/) |
| **Hurricane season (coupling)** | ± complex | Saharan dust suppresses Atlantic cyclogenesis while delivering PM burden |

## 10. Coastal Bend PM10 — what we have

- Total PM10 rows in `aq_coastal_bend`: **~16,600**
- Sites: CC Holly is the primary; CC Dona Park has intermittent
  coverage; Kingsville PM10 is essentially absent
- 24-hr FRM sites outside Coastal Bend (for reference): San Antonio
  Palo Alto 480290060 has 636 rows in `pollutant_daily_24hr` (Bexar,
  not Coastal Bend)
- **2024 NAAQS exceedances (150 µg/m³ 24-hr): NONE in our data** —
  consistent with historical Coastal Bend behavior (sea salt +
  background dust rarely push above 100 µg/m³ absent a Saharan
  intrusion or local dust event)

## 11. Why PM10 still matters for our study

1. Sea salt is a first-order coarse-mode source that PM2.5 measurement
   misses. Any Coastal Bend aerosol characterization that ignores
   coarse fraction is incomplete.
2. Episodic Saharan dust delivers biological + metal cargo with
   documented respiratory-inflammatory consequences.
3. Local drought + agricultural dust events are increasing under
   recurrent La Niña forcing.
4. Port + Ship Channel fugitive dust is a local exposure with
   environmental-justice implications that a PM2.5-only network
   cannot resolve.

**Recommendation for the manuscript:** emphasize both the coverage
limitation and the scientific importance. Advocate for expanded
coarse-fraction monitoring rather than treating PM10 as a legacy
metric.
