## 0. The discontinuity in one sentence

PM10 at CC Holly is a **1-in-6 day filter method (141) from 2015-2018**,
then a **5-year gap (2019-2023)**, then **continuous BAM (639) from
2024 onward.** These two eras must be analyzed separately.

*(TO FILL)*: Known biases:
- Filter method reports true 24-hr integrated mass (gold standard for
  regulatory comparison).
- BAM reports hourly β-attenuation mass estimate; can be 5-15% biased
  vs FRM under high-humidity or high-volatility conditions common in
  Coastal Bend.

## 4. NAAQS

- **24-hr: 150 µg/m³, form = not to exceed more than once per year on
  average over 3 years.**
- **No annual PM10 standard** (revoked in 2006).

CC Holly 2024 exceedances = **0** in the current data.

## 6. Analysis strategy

Given the 5-year gap and the method incompatibility, my recommendation:

- **Era 1 (2015–2018, filter method):** Restrict to descriptive stats;
  ~245 24-hr integrated samples per site-year across 4 years.
  Comparable to regulatory NAAQS assessment.
- **Era 2 (2024+, continuous BAM):** Full hourly analysis viable;
  can support machine-learning models.
- **Do not join.** Cross-era trend claims are indefensible without either
  co-located side-by-side calibration data (we don't have) or explicit
  regulatory correction factors from EPA/TCEQ (we should ask about).
