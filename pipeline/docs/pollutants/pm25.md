# PM2.5 — Fine Particulate Matter

> **Lead:** Aidan Meyers
> **Source deck:** [`briefings/2026-07-01_AM_PM_VOC_briefing.pptx`](../briefings/2026-07-01_AM_PM_VOC_briefing.pptx) (slides 16–29)
> **Coastal Bend data:** 5 sites (Kingsville, CC West, CC Tuloso, CC Dona Park, CC Holly); ~450k hourly rows across 2015–2025
> **Neon:** `aq_coastal_bend.pollutant_hourly WHERE pollutant_group='PM2.5'`

The most-studied pollutant in air-quality epidemiology and — after
Feb 2024's NAAQS tightening from 12 → 9 µg/m³ — the most consequential
for Coastal Bend regulatory work.

## 1. Definition

**PM2.5** = airborne particulate matter with **aerodynamic diameter ≤ 2.5 µm** —
the fraction small enough to penetrate deep into the alveolar region of
the lung, where gas exchange occurs
([40 CFR Part 50, Appendix L](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-40/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-50/appendix-Appendix%20L%20to%20Part%2050)).

Aerodynamic diameter ≠ physical diameter. It's the diameter of a
hypothetical unit-density (1 g/cm³) sphere with the same terminal
settling velocity as the actual particle — a physical standardization
across irregularly-shaped particles.

**PM2.5 is a MIXTURE, not a molecule.** Composition varies seasonally,
geographically, and with source-mix. Typical Coastal Bend contributors:

| Constituent | Origin | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sulfate (SO₄²⁻) | SO2 oxidation from refineries + marine bunker fuel | Dominant secondary component |
| Organic carbon (OC) | Primary + SOA from VOC oxidation | Refinery corridor SOA important |
| Elemental carbon (EC) | Diesel exhaust (port, trucking) | Strong industrial marker |
| Nitrate (NO₃⁻) | NOx → HNO3 → NH4NO3 | Largely gas-phase in Coastal Bend heat |
| Sea salt (Na⁺, Cl⁻, Mg²⁺) | Gulf whitecap bubble-bursting | Coastal baseline |
| Ammonium (NH₄⁺) | Neutralizes acidic sulfate + nitrate | Agricultural upwind |
| Trace metals | Marine bunker fuel (V, Ni), Ship Channel | ([Bozlaker et al. 2013](https://doi.org/10.1021/es402278y)) |

## 2. Sources — primary + secondary

**Primary anthropogenic:**

- Diesel exhaust from the [Port of Corpus Christi](https://portofcc.com/)
  — 3rd-largest US port by tonnage, largest crude oil export port; heavy
  diesel trucking on I-37 and SH-358
- Refinery + petrochemical primary emissions (6 major refineries in
  Nueces County): EC + OC + metals
- Marine bunker fuel combustion — vanadium, nickel signatures
  ([Bozlaker, Spada, Fraser, Chellam 2013, *ES&T*](https://doi.org/10.1021/es402278y))
- Biomass burning (agricultural, Central American fires spring)

**Primary natural:**

- Sea salt aerosol (dominant coastal baseline) — Na, Cl, Mg, SO4 from
  DMS oxidation
- Wildfire smoke — episodic western US in summer; Central American
  spring fires ([Burke et al. 2023, *PNAS*](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2309325120))
- Crustal mineral dust — trans-Atlantic Saharan transport peaks JJA
  ([Prospero & Lamb 2003, *Science*](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1089915);
  [Yu et al. 2015, *RSE*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rse.2015.01.010))

**Secondary formation:**

- **Sulfate**: SO2 + OH → H2SO4(g) + heterogeneous aqueous pathways
- **Nitrate**: NOx → HNO3 → NH4NO3 (largely gas-phase in Coastal Bend heat)
- **Secondary organic aerosol (SOA)** from aromatic + biogenic VOCs —
  coupled to refinery-corridor VOC chemistry (see [VOCs deep-dive](./vocs.md))
- Ammonium neutralizes acidic sulfate + nitrate

**Coastal Bend transport pathways:** SE Gulf flow brings sea salt +
long-range Saharan dust; sea-breeze convergence recirculates Ship
Channel plumes over Hillcrest/Dona Park/Holly; nocturnal land breeze
traps emissions at low mixing height along the coast.

## 3. Health effects — landmark cohort evidence

| Study | Cohort | Result |
|---|---|---|
| [Dockery et al. 1993, *NEJM*](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJM199312093292401) | Six U.S. cities | First large-cohort mortality signal |
| [Pope et al. 2002, *JAMA*](https://doi.org/10.1001/jama.287.9.1132) | ACS, 1.2M adults | Confirmed + scaled |
| [Krewski et al. 2009, HEI Report 140](https://www.healtheffects.org/publication/extended-follow-and-spatial-analysis-american-cancer-society-study-linking-particulate) | ACS reanalysis | HEI validation under intense scrutiny |
| [Di et al. 2017, *NEJM*](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa1702747) | Medicare, 60.9M | **Significant mortality below 12 µg/m³** — cited by EPA in the 2024 revision |
| [IARC Monograph 109 (2013)](https://publications.iarc.who.int/537) | Global synthesis | Outdoor air + PM = **Group 1 carcinogen** (lung cancer) |
| [GBD 2019, *Lancet*](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(20)30752-2) | Global burden | ~4.1 M annual PM2.5 deaths worldwide |

**Organ-system findings:**

- **Cardiovascular (causal, [AHA 2010 statement](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIR.0b013e3181dbece1))** —
  MI, ischemic stroke, heart failure via autonomic dysregulation,
  systemic inflammation, endothelial dysfunction, accelerated
  atherosclerosis.
- **Respiratory + lung cancer** — COPD exacerbation, asthma, LRTI;
  IARC 2013 Group 1.
- **Neurological** — accelerated cognitive decline
  ([Chen et al. 2017, *Lancet*](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(16)32399-6));
  dementia meta-analysis
  ([Wilker et al. 2023, *BMJ*](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj-2022-071620))
  HR 1.07 per 2 µg/m³.
- **Prenatal / perinatal (environmental-justice relevant):** Bekkar et al.
  2020 ([*JAMA Netw Open*](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.8243))
  reviewing 32.8M US births: preterm birth ↑ in 79% of studies (19/24);
  low birth weight ↑ in 86% (25/29). Black mothers + asthmatic populations
  at highest risk — **directly maps onto the Corpus Christi Ship Channel
  neighborhoods (Hillcrest/Dona Park/Holly)** our sites monitor.

## 4. NAAQS — the Feb 2024 tightening

| Year | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1997 | 65 µg/m³ 24-hr · 15 µg/m³ annual | First PM2.5 NAAQS |
| 2006 | 35 µg/m³ 24-hr · 15 µg/m³ annual | 24-hr tightened |
| 2012 | 35 · **12 µg/m³ annual** | Annual tightened |
| **2024** | 35 · **9 µg/m³ annual** | [89 FR 16202](https://www.federalregister.gov/d/2024-02637), effective 2024-05-06; cites Di 2017 |
| WHO 2021 | 15 · **5 µg/m³ annual** | [WHO Global AQ Guidelines](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240034228) |

**2025 note:** EPA announced reconsideration of the 9 µg/m³ standard;
**9.0 remains in effect** as of mid-2026 pending rulemaking. Track
via [EPA PM NAAQS page](https://www.epa.gov/pm-pollution/national-ambient-air-quality-standards-naaqs-pm).

**Design value form:** 3-year rolling average of annual means (annual
standard) OR 3-year avg of the 98th percentile of 24-hr means
(24-hr standard) at a design-value-eligible monitor.

## 5. Measurement methods

### 5.1 FRM — Federal Reference Method (Appendix L)

Filter-based, 24-hr integrated sample. 16.67 L/min flow-controlled
sampler pulls ambient air through a WINS or VSCC 2.5 µm size-selective
inlet; particulate deposits on a 47 mm PTFE Teflon filter over 24 h;
filter equilibrated in the lab at 20–23 °C and 30–40% RH for 24 h before
and after sampling; mass difference measured on a microbalance
sensitive to ≤ 1 µg. Concentration reported at local ambient conditions.

Reference-designated samplers: BGI PQ200 (single-filter), Thermo
Partisol 2025i (sequential), R&P Partisol 2025 (legacy).

**Strengths:** direct mass, gold-standard regulatory yardstick,
composition can be analyzed post-hoc.
**Limitations:** 1 sample per day per site, manual filter handling +
weighing, typical 1-in-3 or 1-in-6 day EPA sampling schedule,
semi-volatile organic losses possible.

### 5.2 FEM — Federal Equivalent Method (continuous)

**BAM 1020 / 1022 (Beta Attenuation Monitor)** — TCEQ's workhorse for
continuous PM2.5. Ambient air → 2.5 µm cyclone → filter tape;
Carbon-14 β-source + Geiger-Müller detector measure beta attenuation
across the loaded spot; mass derived by Beer-Lambert.

| Instrument | EPA designation | Common method code |
|---|---|---|
| Met One BAM 1020 (SCC inlet) | [EQPM-0798-122](https://www.epa.gov/amtic/air-monitoring-methods-criteria-pollutants) | 122 |
| Met One BAM 1020 (VSCC inlet) | EQPM-0308-170 | 170 |
| Met One BAM 1022 (trace) | EQPM-0209-227 | 227 |

**TEOM 1405-F with FDMS** — [Thermo Scientific TEOM 1405-F](https://www.thermofisher.com/order/catalog/product/TEOM1405F).
Particulate deposits on a filter atop an oscillating tapered element;
resonant frequency shift → mass. The FDMS (Filter Dynamics Measurement
System) adds a dry/denuder cycle to correct for the semi-volatile loss
that killed the old TEOM 1400 FEM designation ~2018.
Designation EQPM-0609-181 → method code 181. ~3× the capital cost of
a BAM 1020.

**Optical:** [GRIMM EDM 180](https://www.grimm-aerosol.com/) (laser
scattering, size-resolved, EQPM-0311-195), Thermo 5030 SHARP
(combined nephelometer + BAM).

**Low-cost sensors (PurpleAir etc.) are NOT FEM.** Documented positive
bias at high RH — problematic in Corpus Christi's chronic 70–80% RH.
Not eligible for regulatory NAAQS. EPA publishes a
[PurpleAir correction equation](https://www.epa.gov/air-sensor-toolbox/technical-approaches-sensor-data-airnow-fire-and-smoke-map)
if used for supplementary spatial coverage.

## 6. Parameter codes — the 88101 vs 88502 distinction

**The single most important operational takeaway for anyone querying
`aq_coastal_bend.pollutant_hourly` for PM2.5:**

| Code | Name | Eligible for design value? | Included in `aq_coastal_bend`? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **88101** | PM2.5 – Local Conditions | ✅ YES (FRM + Class I FEM) | Yes |
| **88502** | Acceptable PM2.5 AQI & Speciation Mass | ❌ No (Class II/III continuous only) | Yes |
| 88500 | PM2.5 – Total Atmospheric | Rare in TX | — |

**Query rule:** for any NAAQS-comparable analysis, filter
`WHERE parameter_code = 88101`. For general trend / episode /
weather-correlation work, use both — but always retain `parameter_code`
as a covariate so you can subset later.

Full parameter reference: [EPA AQS parameter codes](https://aqs.epa.gov/aqsweb/documents/codetables/parameters.html)
or query `aq_coastal_bend.parameter_reference`.

## 7. Method-code audit query

Any AQSID with more than one method_code has had a sensor change —
must be documented in methods.

```sql
SELECT aqsid, site_name, method_code,
       COUNT(*) AS n_rows,
       MIN(date_local) AS first, MAX(date_local) AS last
FROM   aq_coastal_bend.pollutant_hourly
WHERE  pollutant_group = 'PM2.5'
GROUP  BY aqsid, site_name, method_code
ORDER  BY aqsid, method_code;
```

Cross-reference with the [EPA AMTIC designated methods list](https://www.epa.gov/amtic/air-monitoring-methods-criteria-pollutants)
to identify each instrument.

## 8. Sensor evolution 2010–2025

- 2010–2013: TCEQ pilot BAM 1020 deployments
- ~2013: **BAM 1020 becomes TCEQ CAMS standard**
- ~2015: BAM 1022 begins rollout (trace-level precision)
- 2018: **EPA withdraws TEOM 1400 FEM designation** (semi-volatile bias)
- 2018–2022: TEOM 1405-F/FDMS selective deployment
- 2020+: Low-cost PurpleAir networks proliferate in Corpus (not FEM)
- **2024-02: PM2.5 annual NAAQS tightened 12 → 9 µg/m³** — moves
  multiple Nueces sites into exceedance
- 2025: EPA announces reconsideration; 9 µg/m³ remains in effect

## 9. Weather relationships

PM2.5 weather regressions in the US explain up to 50% of daily variance
([Tai, Mickley & Jacob 2010, *AE*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.atmosenv.2010.06.060)).
Coastal Bend specifics:

| Variable | Direction | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Wind speed** | ± mixed | Dilutes local emissions but transports sea salt + Saharan dust |
| **Wind direction** | ± strong (source attribution) | ESE = marine/Saharan; ENE = Ship Channel plume; NW = urban |
| **Boundary layer height** | − (higher BL → dilution) | Sea-breeze creates internal structure; nocturnal trap |
| **Temperature** | ± complex | Accelerates SOA formation; shifts NH4NO3 to gas phase ([Dawson, Adams, Pandis 2007, *ACP*](https://doi.org/10.5194/acp-7-4295-2007)) |
| **Humidity** | + (measurement artifact) | Sulfate/nitrate/ammonium hygroscopic growth at RH > 70% inflates BAM + optical readings |
| **Sea-breeze cycle** | ± dominant Coastal Bend forcing | Recirculates Ship Channel plumes over inland receptors |

Conditional bivariate probability functions (CBPF) at
CC Hillcrest/Dona Park/Holly should show characteristic rose alignments
with the Ship Channel bearing during industrial episodes and ESE
alignment during Saharan events.
