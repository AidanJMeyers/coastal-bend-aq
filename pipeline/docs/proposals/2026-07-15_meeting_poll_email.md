I'm writing on behalf of the Melaram Lab Coastal Bend Air Quality team.
At today's meeting (2026-07-15) we converged on a project direction
that we'd love your methodological input on before we commit to it in
earnest.

**Short version of the idea.** Combine wind-direction data with
pollutant flow from **Refinery Row** in Nueces County into a directional
exposure axis, then predict residentially-zoned respiratory +
cardiovascular exacerbations at day / week / month temporal resolution.
It's a machine-learning study anchored on a real industrial point
source — not a diffuse regional correlation. Jasmine's pollution-rose
insight and Manasa's refinery-centering suggestion crystallized into
this at today's meeting.

We think this has:

- **Genuine novelty** — five-dimensional exposure attribution (zone ×
  time × wind vector × pollutant × outcome) that we haven't found in
  the existing literature.
- **Direct policy leverage** — refinery-corridor communities' health
  outcomes as a function of *when* the wind blows industrial pollution
  over them. Zoning + monitoring + permitting implications either way
  the result cuts.
- **Grant leverage** — Dr. Melaram is meeting NIHS on Friday about the
  R25; this project design directly addresses the feasibility critique
  from the climate-change seed grant that scored high on novelty +
  significance.

**What we'd want from a joint session with the two of you** (30–45 min):

1. **Methodological sanity-check** on the exposure-axis construct —
   directional cone from Refinery Row, wind-vector-weighted effective
   concentration, temporal lag Δ between plume arrival and outcome.
2. **Model-family recommendation** — gradient-boosted trees vs temporal
   CNN vs hierarchical Bayesian, or something we haven't considered.
3. **Directionality encoding** for the ML model — continuous angle vs
   binned sectors vs polar embedding.
4. **Validation strategy** — hold-out year, spatial cross-validation by
   zone, or both.

We've published the full scope document at
[aidanjmeyers.github.io/coastal-bend-aq/proposals/refinery_row_directional_health/](https://aidanjmeyers.github.io/coastal-bend-aq/proposals/refinery_row_directional_health/)
